This web page presents instructions for participating in human subject studies conducted at UCLA related to the project "Learning and Communicating Explainable Representations for Analytics and Autonomy".



Instructions

We developed a computer vision system, called Machine M, that was trained to detect and locate human body parts in any given image. In the current experiment, you will first go through a familiarization stage and then a testing stage. First, in the familiarization stage, you will observe 5 sample images and evaluations on the machine M's corresponding performance. Your task is to understand what the machine M can or cannot do. Then, in the testing stage, we will ask questions to test your understanding of the machine M's behavior.

(a) Familiarization Stage: In the familiarization stage, we will show you images that were provided to the machine M. The images contain one human figure with the entire body.

You will see the machine M's performance on detecting and locating human body parts of the human in these images. For some images, the machine M is able to accurately detect and locate human body parts. However, in other images, the machine M may not be able to accurately detect or locate human body parts.

You are expected to observe the machine M's performance and understand its behavior (e.g. how the machine M functions and what it can or cannot do). For each trial, you will be given 45 seconds to observe the machine M's behavior. Please patiently view the provided information and pay attention during the whole 45 seconds. It will automatically move on to the next trial after 45 seconds.

(b) Testing Stage: After you finish the familiarization stage, we will test your understanding of the machine M's behavior. We will show you a new set of images and tasks and ask several questions such as "Will the machine M correctly solve the task?", "What would be the output of the machine M", etc. Please choose your answers based on your understanding of the machine M's behavior.
